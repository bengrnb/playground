About Me

I'm a master's degree student at the University of Michigan's School of Information, where I study Social Computing, Human Computer Interaction and User Experience Research and Design. With an undergraduate background in psychology and entrepreneurship, user-centered product design connects my curiosity about human behavior, my interest in improving well-being, and my passion for innovating new technologies and products. Through my work, I aim to make technology more accessible and easier to use for the widest range of users and to help people connect with each other, information and services that will improve the quality of their lives.

Inspiration

  • Since I was a child, I have always enjoyed creating and building new things. Whether it be Legos, a 3D printed phone case, a smart home system to look after my grandmother who suffered with dementia, or even this very portfolio, I have felt the excitement of putting in time and working hard to create something that is meaningful and enhancing to myself and others.

    Doing UX work was a natural path for me that I have found both enjoyable and challenging. My passion for UX was ignited and evolved over the course of an internship at the University of Michigan's Information and Technology Services (ITS) department. During the internship, I was involved in a project to redesign the University's Psychological Counseling Services (CAPS) website to help make mental health services more accessible to students. In my interactions with students, faculty, and mental health professionals, I was able to see firsthand how important UX work was, and the power it can have to make a difference.
